Our Mission

Banking That Belongs to Everyone

Harrison Financial was founded on a simple premise: the 47 million underbanked Americans deserve better than what traditional banking offers them. Too many people pay hundreds of dollars a year in maintenance fees, overdraft charges, and ATM penalties—to banks that make billions in profits.

We built a digital-first banking platform that eliminates those fees entirely, combines best-in-class savings rates with powerful spending tools, and wraps it all in the security infrastructure that financial institutions twice our size can barely match.

We're not just a tech startup that plays at banking. We're a regulated financial services company with a genuine partner bank, FDIC insurance, and a compliance program built to the same standards as the biggest names on Wall Street.

Our Story

From Idea to 3 Million Members

2018
Harrison Financial Founded
Eleanor Harrison and Marcus Reid incorporate the company in Delaware. Seed funding of $4.2M from Sequoia and Andreessen Horowitz.
2019
Beta Launch & Series A
Private beta opens to 10,000 users. Banking partnership finalized with Meridian Bank, N.A. Series A closes at $28M.
2020
Public Launch & 250K Members
Public launch in all 50 states. Reached 250,000 members within 8 months. Launched high-yield savings at 1.5% APY.
2021
1 Million Members & Series B
Crossed 1M members. Launched personal lending. Series B: $120M led by Tiger Global. Expanded team to 300 employees.
2022
Lending & Credit Cards
Launched the Harrison Credit Card and Credit Builder Loan. Reached $800M in deposits under management.
2023
Series C & 2M Members
Series C: $250M at a $1.8B valuation. Crossed 2M members. Named to Fast Company's Most Innovative Companies list.
2024
3M Members & $2.4B in Deposits
Reached 3 million active members and $2.4B in deposits. Raised savings APY to 4.85%. Expanded team to 850+ globally.
2025
IPO Preparation & Platform Expansion
Actively exploring IPO timeline. Launched enterprise API program and expanded into business banking beta.
