About SecureStar

We build the fraud infrastructure that banks can actually explain

SecureStar was founded by engineers who spent years inside fintech and ML platforms watching the same problem repeat: companies with real fraud risk, and vendors with real solutions, unable to find each other — or trust each other once found.

Our Mission

Fraud detection that's fast, honest, and auditable

Most fraud platforms are built by data scientists who think in model performance metrics. SecureStar was built by engineers who also had to sit in front of regulators and explain why a transaction was declined.

That experience shaped every product decision we've made. Speed matters. A fraud check that adds 200ms to your authorization is a fraud check your users will feel. Explainability matters. A model that can't justify its decisions to a compliance officer is a liability, not an asset. Control matters. One-size-fits-all fraud models don't fit anyone.

We built SecureStar to be the platform we wished existed when we were on the other side of the table.

Our Story

From pain point to platform

💡
2019 — The Problem
The realization
Co-founders Priya Nair and James Kowalski are working at separate fintech companies. Both experience the same frustration: fraud vendors with impressive pitch decks that crumble when it's time to explain a decision to a compliance examiner — or meet a sub-100ms latency budget.
🛠️
2020 — Foundation
SecureStar Labs incorporated
Priya and James incorporate SecureStar Labs in San Francisco. Seed round of $4.8M led by Emergence Capital. First 8 engineers hired from Stripe, Palantir, and Two Sigma. Core mission: build a fraud platform that satisfies engineers and compliance teams simultaneously.
🚀
2021 — Product
v1 launches with hybrid engine and explainability
First production version ships with the hybrid ML + rule engine. Early customer Nexo Payments runs parallel tests against their incumbent vendor — SecureStar delivers 31% fewer false positives and sub-60ms p99 latency on their Kafka stack. Series A closes at $18M.
📈
2022 — Scale
Kafka-native connector and Python SDK released
Engineering team ships the Kafka connector and Python SDK, cutting typical integration time from 3–4 weeks to under 2 weeks. Customer count reaches 40. First enterprise AML compliance deployment with full regulatory export pipeline.
🔬
2023 — Research
Custom model tuning API and edge deployment beta
Custom model fine-tuning on customer data launches, eliminating the one-size-fits-all problem. Edge deployment beta achieves sub-20ms p50 for select enterprise customers. SOC 2 Type II certification completed.
🌍
2024–2025 — Expansion
Series B and international expansion
Series B closes at $42M. EU data residency launched for GDPR compliance. Team grows to ~50 people. Platform processes over 3 billion transactions. New features: dynamic rule updates, A/B shadow mode, and FinCEN SAR export for US customers.

Values

What we believe

⚡

Speed is a feature

Latency isn't a technical footnote. In payment authorization, every millisecond is a UX decision. We treat our latency SLA as a product commitment, not an aspiration.

🔍

Opacity is a liability

An AI system that can't explain itself isn't trustworthy — it's a regulatory risk. We believe every automated financial decision should be explainable to the person it affected.

🎛️

Control belongs to the customer

You know your customers better than we do. Your compliance team should own the rules. Your data scientists should tune the model. We provide the infrastructure; you make the decisions.

🤝

Integration should be boring

The best vendor integration is one that takes days, not months, and doesn't require a migration project. We obsess over our SDK ergonomics, connector simplicity, and zero-rework deployments.
